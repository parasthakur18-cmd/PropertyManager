Here are the final business rules. Follow exactly.

---

### Q1 — Expense Blocking Level

Check MUST be on the selected wallet only (NOT total property balance).

Example:
Cash = 500, UPI = 10,000
Expense from Cash = 2,000

→ BLOCK (because Cash wallet is insufficient)

Do NOT combine wallet balances.

---

### Q2 — Cross-Wallet Transfers

Transfers must be SAME wallet type only:

Cash → Cash
UPI/Bank → UPI/Bank

Do NOT allow Cash → UPI or UPI → Cash via transfer.

---

### Q3 — Partial Transfers

YES — partial transfers are allowed.

Example:
Property B has ₹7,000
Property A needs ₹10,000

→ Allow transfer of ₹7,000
→ User can transfer remaining from another property

---

### Q4 — PnL Treatment

Transfers must NOT affect PnL.

Correct:

Profit = Revenue − Expenses

Transfers should be tracked separately as:

* Transfer In
* Transfer Out

They must NOT be included in revenue or expense.

---

### Q5 — Daily Closing

Transfers should be allowed even if previous day is closed.

They must be recorded in the CURRENT open day only.

Do NOT modify closed day balances.

---

### Q6 — Approval System

No approval required.

Transfers are instant for authorized users.

---

### Q7 — Reversibility

Transfers should be reversible.

DO NOT delete transactions.

Instead:

* Create reverse transactions
* Maintain full audit trail

Example:
Original:
B → -10,000
A → +10,000

Reverse:
B → +10,000
A → -10,000

---

### Q8 — Concurrency / Safety

YES — implement database-level protection.

Use:

* DB transaction (atomic)
* Row-level locking OR safe balance check

This is mandatory to prevent negative balances.

---

### FINAL RULES

* Do NOT break existing wallet system

* Do NOT modify existing sources

* Add new source types:

  * internal_transfer
  * external_funding

* Transfers are NOT income

* Transfers are NOT expense

* Transfers are balance movement only

* Expense must NEVER make wallet negative
