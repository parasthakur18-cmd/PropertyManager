In the current flow, the “Advance” section is creating confusion for checked-in guests.

Right now, when we manually add advance payment from the “Edit Booking” option (especially cash collected at the time of check-in), the status is still showing as “Pending.” However, when payment is collected through the online payment link, it correctly shows as “Received via UPI.”

What I would suggest is:

* If advance payment is added manually during check-in (Cash / UPI), the system should immediately update the advance status as “Advance Received.”
* The color coding should also change accordingly so the front desk team can instantly identify payment status visually.
* “Pending” should only appear when no advance has actually been received.
* Manual payment entries and online payment link collections should follow the same payment logic and status behavior.
* There should also be a payment mode tag visible, such as:
  • Advance Received (Cash)
  • Advance Received (UPI)
  • Pending Advance

Also, when we receive payment through the “Collect” button via the online payment link and the payment is confirmed, the “Collect” button should automatically disappear or become disabled.

Currently, even after receiving and confirming the payment, the collect link/button is still active and clickable, which may create confusion or result in duplicate payment requests being sent to the guest.

Ideally:

* Once payment is successfully received and confirmed, the “Collect” button should automatically hide or become inactive.
* Clicking the button again after payment confirmation should not resend the payment link.
* The system should clearly show the payment as “Received via UPI” with updated status and color coding.
* Only pending payments should have an active “Collect” button available.

Also, for checked-in guests, the system should clearly differentiate between:

* Advance received
* Partial payment pending
* Fully paid
* No advance received

This will improve visibility for the operations and accounts team, avoid duplicate payment requests, and reduce manual verification work.
